Paul, Disciple of Jesus Christ

Drastic Conversion

Paul was in prison throughout most of his ministry. He wrote almost half of the new testiment behind bars.

Paul's Journey:

  • Birth: Born an Israelite in Tarsus of Celicia
  • Citazenship: Of the tribe of Benjamin, a "Hebrew of the Hebrews" (Phil 3:5) A Roman citizen by birthright
  • Education: Studied at the school of Gamaliel in Jerusalem, educated as a Pharisee
  • Pre-Conversion: His primary goal was to kill or imprison followers of, what was then known as "the Way" or Christ followers
  • Key event: Was present and approving of the murder of the first Christian martyr, Stephen
  • Conversion: While on his way to Demascus to continue his pusecution of the Church, he was visited by "Jesus, whom you are persecuting". He was blinded by the light and told to continue to Demascus where God would provide a profit to heal him
  • Key event: Paul (at the time called Saul) was told by God that his name will now be Paul, and he will be God's voice to the Gentiles (Non Jews and Greeks)
  • Travels: From here, Paul dedicated his life to traveling and telling the Greeks the Good News of Jesus Christ: He didn't just die for the sins of the Jews, but for the sins of the world (John 3:16)

"We walk by faith, not by sight"

-- Paul, the Apostle of Jesus Christ of Nazareth
